Origin of Eukaryotes Endogenous origin of nucleus and ER It was thought that there was an ancestral cell that formed around nucleus, branching led ER. This organism was anaerobic.

Serial Endosymbiosis: Theory originally proposed by Mereschkovsky (1905) and refined by Margulis (1981).

Margulis: Symbiosis driving force in evolution!

Origin of mitochondrion: 2 ideas Prokaryote before. Now, bigger cell engulfed it for food but didn't digest. Because it utilized oxygen, had a more efficient ATP production. Good for both partners. Gave rise to animal, fungi and other heterotrophs.

Origin of chloroplast: Came after mitochondrion. Engulfed it, and stayed because it was beneficial.

Why would you believe mitochondrion evolved first? All cells have mitochondria but not chloroplast.

Primary endosymbiosis led to red alga and green alga lines. Followed by secondary endosymbiosis in red and green algal lines. Can have occurred more than once? Photosynthetic protists are algae. Count chloroplast membranes to see how many times endosymbiosis occurred. Evidence: Based on similarities of extant prokaryotes with mitochondrion and chloroplast. Secondary endosymbiosis has more than 2 chloroplast membranes.
